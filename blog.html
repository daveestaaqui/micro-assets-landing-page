Privacy & Security

Why Privacy-First Extensions Matter More Than Ever (And How to Verify Them)

In 2025 alone, multiple popular Chrome extensions were caught secretly selling browsing data to data brokers. Extensions with millions of users were harvesting every URL visited, every search query typed, and every form field filled.

The problem? Most users never read permissions. They click "Add to Chrome" and assume Google vetted it. But Google's review process is automated and easily gamed.

How to verify an extension is safe:

  • Check the permissions field in the manifest (fewer = better)
  • Look for host_permissions: [""] — this means it can access EVERY page you visit
  • Check if it makes network requests (use DevTools → Network tab)
  • Look for a privacy policy on the developer's website

Every FlowKit extension is built with these principles: minimal permissions, zero network requests, and 100% local execution.
