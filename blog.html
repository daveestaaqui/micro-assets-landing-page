Contracts & Legal

Why 90% of Freelance Developers Sign Illegally Binding NDAs (And How to Stop)

If you are a freelance software engineer or agency owner, you probably sign Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreements (MNDAs) or Master Service Agreements (MSAs) every month. And if you are like 90% of developers, you skim them, assume they are "standard boilerplate," and sign on the dotted line.

This is exactly how you lose your intellectual property.

Corporate lawyers don't write "standard boilerplate." They write terms designed specifically to transfer liability away from the enterprise and onto the freelancer. If a client gets sued because a library you used had a vulnerable zero-day exploit, aggressive indemnification clauses can force you to pay their legal fees out of pocket.

Furthermore, sneaky residuals clauses ("Ideas or concepts retained in the unaided memory of the receiving party...") can silently strip you of the IP you thought you owned.

You aren't a lawyer. Paying a firm $400/hour to review a $5,000 contract destroys your margins.

The Rise of the "Surf-By" ADA Lawsuit in 2026 (And Why Automated Tools Fail)

Accessibility isn't just a moral imperative anymore—it's a massive legal liability. In recent years, "surf-by" ADA lawsuits have skyrocketed. Law firms use automated scrapers to find e-commerce sites and SaaS platforms missing fundamental WCAG 2.1 requirements, followed by immediate settlement demand letters.

Most development teams rely on heavy, expensive desktop suites or generic browser auditors that inject messy HTML into the DOM just to test it, breaking the layout. Others rely on automated overlays (like accessiBe) which courts have repeatedly ruled do not protect you from liability.

To truly protect your clients, developers must audit contrast ratios, ARIA roles, and heading structures interactively in the browser during the staging phase.
